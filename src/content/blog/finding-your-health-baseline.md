Most people come to health coaching with a destination in mind. They want to lose weight, sleep better, have more energy, resolve a chronic symptom. The goal is clear; the path forward feels murky. And this is exactly where most wellness efforts stumble before they even begin.

We skip the baseline.

---

## What Does "Baseline" Actually Mean?

Your health baseline is a snapshot of where your body, mind, and habits are *right now* — before any interventions, supplements, dietary changes, or coaching work. It encompasses more than a number on a scale or a cholesterol reading, though those matter too.

A meaningful baseline includes:

- **Objective markers** — blood work, blood pressure, resting heart rate, sleep duration, HRV if you track it
- **Functional markers** — energy levels throughout the day, digestion, mood stability, cognitive clarity
- **Lifestyle foundations** — what you're actually eating (not what you think you're eating), sleep habits, stress load, movement patterns
- **Contextual factors** — life circumstances, relationships, work demands, seasonal rhythms

Without this full picture, any wellness plan is essentially a guess. You might pick the right interventions. You might not. And you'll have no objective way to know whether anything is actually working.

---

## The Problem With Jumping to Goals

Here's what typically happens: someone decides they want to "eat healthier." They overhaul their diet overnight. Two weeks in, they feel better — maybe. Or they feel worse before they feel better, and they don't know if that's normal. They can't tell if the fatigue they're experiencing is detox, under-eating, stress, or just a bad week.

They have no baseline to compare to.

Three months later, they've lost momentum. They've abandoned the changes. They return to their old habits, assuming the experiment failed — even if it was actually working.

**A baseline would have changed everything.** It would have given them reference points to track real progress, adjust course intelligently, and stay motivated through the inevitable rough patches.

## Establishing Your Baseline: A Practical Starting Point

You don't need to run every possible lab test before making positive changes. But here are the most valuable places to start:

**1. A food journal (3–5 days, honest)**  
Not calorie-counted, not judged — just observed. What are you actually eating, when, and how do you feel afterward? Patterns emerge quickly.

**2. A sleep log**  
Total hours, subjective quality, how rested you feel at wake. This alone reveals more than most biomarkers.

**3. An energy map**  
Chart your energy at two-hour intervals for a week. Where are the crashes? The peaks? Is there a predictable rhythm — or is it chaos?

**4. Core bloodwork**  
A comprehensive metabolic panel, CBC, fasting glucose and insulin, thyroid panel, and Vitamin D at minimum. These give you a biological foundation to work from.

**5. A stress and mood check-in**  
How often do you feel anxious, irritable, or overwhelmed? How do these states affect your eating, sleep, and motivation to take care of yourself?

## Why This Changes Everything

When you begin with a baseline, something shifts. You stop working *against* your body and start working *with* it. You replace guesswork with data — and data, in health coaching, is liberation.

It also keeps you honest. We tend to remember how bad things were worse than they actually were once we feel better, or minimize progress when we're in a plateau. Your baseline is the truth you return to when memory gets fuzzy.

Most importantly: a baseline reminds you that this is *your* health story. Not a generic protocol. Not someone else's transformation. Yours.
