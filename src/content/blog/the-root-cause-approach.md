You've probably had this experience: you go to the doctor with a complaint — fatigue, bloating, skin issues, low mood, recurring headaches — and you leave with something to *manage* the symptom. A prescription. A referral. A suggestion to change one thing.

The symptom quiets down. Sometimes. For a while.

But it comes back. Or another symptom takes its place. And you find yourself in a cycle — treating, managing, suppressing — without ever feeling truly well.

This is the fundamental limitation of a symptom-focused approach to health. And it's why root-cause health coaching exists.

---

## What Is Root-Cause Health Coaching?

Root-cause coaching starts from a different premise entirely: **symptoms are signals, not problems**.

When your body presents a symptom — inflammation, fatigue, digestive distress, mood dysregulation — it is communicating. It is telling you that something upstream has gone wrong. The symptom is the messenger. Suppressing it is not the same as solving it.

A root-cause approach asks:

- *Why is this happening?*
- *What systems or inputs are driving this outcome?*
- *What is the body trying to adapt to?*
- *What would have to be different for this symptom to resolve on its own?*

This requires a willingness to look at the whole person — not just the symptom — and to trace patterns across multiple domains of health.

---

## The Difference in Practice

Here's a concrete example. Imagine someone struggling with persistent afternoon fatigue and low mood. A symptom-management approach might recommend coffee, an iron supplement, or screening for depression.

A root-cause approach asks different questions:

- How is this person sleeping? (Quality, not just duration)
- What are their blood sugar patterns throughout the day?
- Are there signs of adrenal dysfunction from prolonged stress?
- What is their thyroid function, including free T3 and T4 — not just TSH?
- How is their gut microbiome? Are they absorbing nutrients effectively?
- What are the demands on their time and attention — is there a chronic draining of resources?

The fatigue and low mood are real. But they may be the final symptom of a chain of upstream imbalances. Address those, and the symptoms often resolve — not because they were suppressed, but because the body no longer needs to signal distress.

## Why This Approach Takes More Time

Root-cause work is not quick-fix work. This is worth saying plainly, because the wellness industry has become very good at selling speed.

Understanding your full health picture — the labs, the history, the lifestyle patterns, the environmental factors — takes time. Building sustainable changes across multiple systems takes time. The body adapts slowly, and real healing is not linear.

But here's the tradeoff: symptom management is *also* slow. It's just slow in a circle. You manage the same issues year after year, often requiring more and more intervention over time to achieve the same result.

Root-cause work is slow in a direction. It compounds. Each change you make creates conditions for the next change to be easier. You are building a foundation, not patching a hole.

## What Root-Cause Coaching Looks Like at Piretti Health

In our work together, we begin by understanding the full upstream picture of your health. This includes:

**Your personal history** — not just medical history, but the story of your body. When did you start feeling this way? What has changed? What have you already tried?

**Your current lifestyle inputs** — sleep, nutrition, movement, stress, connection. These are not peripheral to health; they are the primary drivers of it.

**Relevant lab work** — we review what you have, and identify what gaps exist in your objective data.

**The patterns** — health rarely presents as an isolated issue. We look for themes, connections, and compounding factors.

From there, we build a plan that addresses the upstream causes — not the downstream signals. This might mean working on blood sugar regulation before tackling energy. It might mean addressing gut health before working on skin. It might mean restructuring sleep before addressing mood.

The order matters. The connections matter. You matter — as a whole person, not a collection of symptoms to be managed.

## A Different Question

The simplest way I can describe the root-cause approach is this:

Symptom management asks: *How do we make this problem smaller?*

Root-cause coaching asks: *Why does this problem exist at all — and what would have to be true for it to stop existing?*

That second question leads somewhere different. Somewhere better.
